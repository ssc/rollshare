Phone Connection Help

Having trouble getting your phone to connect to RollShare? Work through these tips from top to bottom.

Most Common Fixes
📶
Make sure your phone is on the same WiFi as your laptop
RollShare connects directly over your local network — both devices must be on the same WiFi network. If your laptop is on one network and your phone is on another (or on mobile data), they can't reach each other.
📄
Close extra browser tabs pointing to the RollShare camera page
If your phone browser has more than one tab open to the RollShare camera page, the tabs will compete for the camera and WebRTC connection. Close all but one.
🔄
Toggle your phone's WiFi off and back on
Stale WiFi state can prevent a new connection from establishing. Turn WiFi off on your phone, wait a couple of seconds, and turn it back on. Then re-scan the QR code.
Fallback Options
📱
Use your phone as a WiFi hotspot and have your laptop join it
This is the most reliable workaround if your router is causing problems. Enable Mobile Hotspot on your phone, connect your laptop to it, then restart RollShare. Your phone and laptop will be on a direct network with no router in the way.

Note: your laptop will use your phone's mobile data while connected to the hotspot.
Other Things to Check
🚫
Avoid guest or public WiFi networks
Many guest and public WiFi networks enable AP isolation, which prevents devices on the same network from talking to each other directly. Use a personal home or office WiFi network instead.
🛡️
Check your VPN and firewall
If you're running a VPN, try disconnecting it — VPNs often route traffic in a way that blocks local device discovery. Also make sure Windows Firewall isn't blocking the RollShare companion app (look for a firewall prompt on first launch and click Allow).
🔁
Re-scan the QR code if you've been waiting a while
The connection URL includes your laptop's local IP address. If that address changed (e.g. after a reconnect or sleep), the old QR code won't work — just scan the current one shown in the app.
🌐
Use Chrome or Safari on your phone
RollShare uses WebRTC for the camera stream. Chrome (Android) and Safari (iOS) have the best WebRTC support. Firefox may work but can behave differently; other browsers are untested.
📷
Allow camera permission in your browser
When you first open the RollShare camera page, your browser will ask for camera access. Make sure you tap Allow. If you previously denied it, go to your browser's site settings and reset the permission.
