Terms & Privacy

Last updated: May 2026

The short version

When you sign in to RollShare, we use your email address only to identify you across your devices and to send you transactional or product messages from RollShare. We never sell your email, and we never share it with third parties for marketing.

Why we ask for your email

  • To key your app data and settings. Your email (linked to a stable user ID) is how RollShare knows which preferences, saved layouts, and configuration belong to you on this computer and on any future device.
  • To send you the sign-in code. RollShare uses email codes (one-time passwords) instead of passwords. We email you a 6-digit code each time you need to verify a new device.
  • To contact you about RollShare. Occasional messages directly from us about meaningful updates, important fixes, or things you specifically asked about. Low volume — not a marketing newsletter.

What we do not do

  • We do not sell your email address.
  • We do not share your email with third-party advertisers, data brokers, or marketing partners.
  • We do not read or store the contents of your video feed. Your camera and microphone stay on your local network — RollShare never uploads your video to our servers.

How your email is stored

Authentication is handled by Supabase, a service that securely stores your email and a stable user ID for us. The sign-in code is delivered through a transactional email provider whose only job is to deliver mail; they do not use your address for anything else.

Both providers may process your email under their own privacy policies as data sub-processors, strictly for the purpose of delivering RollShare's sign-in flow.

Anonymous usage data

RollShare collects anonymous product analytics — things like "the app launched," "the camera started," "pairing succeeded" — tied to your stable user ID. This helps us spot bugs and figure out which platforms work well. It does not include your video, your audio, the contents of your screen, or anything you type.

Dice tray images and clips

RollShare may capture still images and short video clips of your dice camera feed in order to recognise and track dice. These captures may be used by RollShare for training, statistics, or publicly for marketing or demonstration purposes.

We do not believe dice camera captures contain meaningful personally identifiable information or are that "private" — they are close-up shots of dice and the surface they rest on. However, if you have any concern about a specific capture, please contact us and we will remove it promptly.

Your choices

  • Sign out at any time from the RollShare app. Your local session is cleared.
  • Delete your account. Email us (see below) and we will remove your email and associated data from our systems.
  • Stop receiving product email from us by replying to any RollShare message and asking. Sign-in codes will continue to be delivered as long as your account is active, since they are required for you to log in.

Contact

Questions, account deletion requests, or anything else: please reach out via the project issue tracker or the contact link on our home page.

RollShare is an early-stage independent project. This page is written in plain language and reflects what we actually do. If our practices change, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date at the top will move forward.
