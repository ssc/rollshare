Publish Rolls

Share your dice results directly to Roll20 or Discord — automatically.

What it does

🎲
One click to share your roll result
After launching Roll20 or Discord from the Publish Rolls panel, RollShare watches for your next roll and automatically posts the result to the chat — including a link to the captured image. No copy-pasting, no switching windows mid-game.

How to use it

1
Open the Publish Rolls panel
Press Ctrl+R or scroll down in the RollShare companion window to the Publish Rolls section. If you don't see it, make sure you are signed in as an Early Access user.
2
Select an app and click Launch
Choose Roll20 or Discord from the dropdown, then click Launch. A browser window will open. Navigate to the campaign or server channel where you want to post rolls.
3
Click "Publish Next Roll…"
Back in the companion panel, click Publish Next Roll… Optionally type a label (e.g. Stealth check) so everyone knows what the roll was for, then click Next.
4
Roll your dice
RollShare will detect your roll automatically. Once detected, it posts the result to chat — either pasting it into the text field (with Auto-click Send checked) or leaving it there for you to review first.

Requirements

📱
Phone must be streaming
Make sure your phone is connected and actively streaming before clicking Publish Next Roll… If it isn't, scan the QR code in the Phone Stream panel and tap Start Streaming.
📐
Die Size Calibration must be complete
If the calibration wizard is showing, complete it first by throwing a die three times as prompted. Calibration only needs to be done once per phone/camera setup.
💬
Navigate to the chat before arming
For Roll20, open your campaign and make sure the chat panel is visible. For Discord, navigate to the channel you want to post in. RollShare probes for the text input when you click Publish Next Roll… — if it can't find it, a message will tell you what to fix.

Tips

🔁
Repeat without re-launching
After a roll is published the panel returns to idle — just click Publish Next Roll… again for your next roll. You only need to re-launch if you close the browser window.
✏️
Use labels to give context
The label field is optional but recommended — it appears alongside the roll result so your party knows what it was for (e.g. Attack roll, Perception check).
